Developed methods for mapping representations in deep neural networks to patterns of brain activity measured with fMRI. This work bridges computational neuroscience and deep learning to better understand how the brain processes visual information.

## Background

Understanding the relationship between artificial and biological neural networks is a key question at the intersection of neuroscience and AI. By systematically comparing learned representations across network layers with neural responses across brain regions, we can gain insight into the computational principles shared between artificial and biological vision systems.

## Approach

Using voxelwise encoding models, we predicted brain activity from deep neural network features. This allowed us to build detailed maps of which network layers best predict activity in different cortical regions, revealing a hierarchical correspondence between network depth and the cortical visual processing stream.

## Results

Our findings showed a systematic mapping between DNN layers and cortical areas, with early layers predicting activity in primary visual cortex and deeper layers predicting activity in higher-level visual areas. This work contributes to our understanding of both biological vision and the representations learned by deep networks.
