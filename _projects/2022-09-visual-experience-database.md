Contributed to the Visual Experience Database Project, a large-scale effort to collect and analyze naturalistic visual experience data. This project aims to characterize the statistics of real-world visual input and understand how the brain is adapted to process it.

## Overview

Most vision research uses carefully controlled stimuli that may not reflect the complexity of everyday visual experience. The Visual Experience Database captures hours of first-person video from participants going about their daily lives, providing a rich dataset for studying naturalistic vision.

## Contributions

Developed analysis pipelines for processing and characterizing the visual statistics present in the database, including spatial frequency content, color distributions, and scene categories. These analyses help bridge the gap between laboratory vision science and real-world visual processing.
